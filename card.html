Maine Coon

The Maine Coon's thick, water-resistant fur and tufted ears help it thrive in cold climates, making it well-suited for its origins in the northeastern United States.

Known for their large size, tufted ears, and bushy tails, Maine Coon cats are one of the most recognizable domestic cat breeds. They are friendly, intelligent, and often referred to as "gentle giants." Their sturdy build and long, shaggy coat were adaptations to survive harsh winters in their native region.
